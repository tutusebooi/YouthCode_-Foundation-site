// mission

To empower youth through skills training, local economic projects, technology, research, entrepreneurship and partnerships that create sustainable community development — built in Thembalethu, for Thembalethu, by the people who live here.

// the problem

Talent isn't the gap. Access is.

01

Youth unemployment

Thembalethu's NEET rate sits far above the national average — not for lack of ability, but for lack of structured opportunity.

02

No dedicated hub

There is currently no youth-focused skills, innovation or enterprise space serving the community directly.

03

Digital divide

Limited access to computers, connectivity and structured digital training keeps young people locked out of remote and online income.

04

Disconnected economy

George's economy is growing — but Thembalethu isn't yet wired into that growth. We aim to build the bridge.

// programmes.json

Eight programmes, phased to build trust before scale

We don't launch everything on day one. Year 1 proves the model with low-capital, high-impact work. Years 2 and 3 unlock bigger ventures once the track record exists to support them.

01

Digital Opportunity & Online Employment

Coding, digital marketing, freelancing and AI-tool training that connects youth to remote jobs and online income beyond Thembalethu's borders.

// roadmap.yml

Three years, building in order

Year 1 · Foundation
  • Register as NPC / NPO
  • Launch Programmes 01–04
  • Build 5+ founding partnerships
  • Establish funding track record
Year 2 · Expansion
  • Secure Innovation Centre facility
  • Certify swim instructors & lifeguards
  • Launch Programmes 05–06
  • Scale Year 1 work from research findings
Year 3 · Scale
  • Launch recycling & agriculture project
  • Launch community bakery
  • Hit 30–40% self-generated income
  • Document model for replication
